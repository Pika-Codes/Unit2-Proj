Why Is Climate Change A Problem?

Climate change is a problem because of the long lasting impacts it causes. The impacts of climate chage are immense and will need to be redued as much as possible. I will go over the problems on the information page, but there are a few major reasons steps haven't been takes to reduce the impact of climate change. This is because there isn't enough governmental support for sustainable power production and increased energy efficiency. This means that sustainable energy is getting nowhere in becoming the main source of energy in all individual countries, which is a crucial step towards fully sustainable energy production. Without fully sustainable energy production, we won't be able to protect this planet from burning up, as evident in the recent Australia situation. There aren't many solutions for this problem for 3 reasons. The first is the technology for power production and transportation isn't where it needs to be for mass sustainable power generation, specifically solar. The second reason is cost. It will cost a lot of money to set up the system, and governments aren't willing to spend money on things that don't appease big companies like oil ones. The final reason is the government itself. The budget is a problem, but to do this, it will need to pass through ALL stages of government. Some people are unwilling for the possibility of either budget cuts or cuts to their own pay, and there are others who are in cahoots with oil companies or people who don't believe climate change os a problem. For this reason, it is a possibility that the proposal will be denied.
